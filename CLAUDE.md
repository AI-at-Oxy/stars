We are creating an AI-powered tutor that teaches students about astronomy. This version of the tutor will teach students to identify constellations in view in southern California in the month of April, and will provide a diverse and multicultural background of each constellation (such as origin myths from several cultures, or that constellation's use in science or navigation). 

Brainstorming for Design Doc

- Problem Statement: children learn best when they are immersed and they can apply knowledge, also (this is where we insert study) studies are revealing that children are spending more time indoors, this gets children outside and engaged with their environments!
- aimed at a slightly younger audience, anyone can use and enjoy
- people who are curious!
- struggle with identifying constellations, engaging with nature
- importance of learning interesting and applicable concepts, getting outside, looking at the world around them
- culturally focused

Functionality

- simple virtual environment the kid can explore, learner led experience
- student can click on a star, gives an explanation, ask more questions, focused on learning about the history of the constellation (and mythology about stars in general) and a bit of scientific background

General Brainstorming:

- start with screen filled with very bright constellations, moving mouse explores/turns around
- “Click on a star to begin”, hover over stars, constellations form, then a drawing from myth
- when clicking on a star, pulls up constellation to fit the whole screen, learner can ask questions
- responses are very story like
- start with a grounding, background fact, informing the learning about what they are learning
- focused on spring constellations
- start with Ursa Major, or another grounding constellation (like pokemon ha)

System Prompt Ideas:

- responses are very story like
- speaking as sherpa of the stars
- Avoid details associated with popular astrology
- incorporating mythology from different cultures

Visual Ideas:

- explorable virtual environment feel, clear graphics
- We will have to choose which constellations/stars to include as to not overcrowd the virtual sky

Run Through of how the app works:

- Brief Intro Statement (about stars, contellations, mythology)
- “Are you familiar with any constellations?” - if not, start with polaris and big dipper
- if student mentions a constellation not in view “We can’t see that right now, but let’s look at this one”
- Good! start with first constellation [Show constellation]
- What do you know anything about this constellation?
- If nothing —> Start with simple facts (how many stars, brief story)
- if something, have responses build on what the learner already knows
- Storytelling gets more layered with more questions/the longer the learner engages with the system
- Show surrounding stars
- Do you recognize any of this?
- Give users room for creativity while also teaching mythology
- Break it down into chunks, “do you see the sword?”
- When user knows very little, counting the stars
- give overview of different cultural stories, ask user
- system prompt includes pulling from different sources, prioritizing certain reputable sources over others
- some mechanism to move on to a new constellation, then there is a quiz
- a couple constellations at a time, then recentering on grounding constellation
- after a certain number of engagements, “let’s review what we’ve learned so far!”
- start with less constellations in view, then review
- only focus on stars that we can see in april in southern california
